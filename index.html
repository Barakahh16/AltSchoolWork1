ABOUT ME

My name is Barakat Ayodimeji. I was born and raised in Nigeria. I am 20 years old and I am currently in my fourth year of university, studying Computer Engineering in University of Ilorin, Nigeria. I love reading, I love nature and I am a big fan of BTS, the popular South Korean boy band. I am a fan of music in general and I spend a lot of my time discovering new artists.

I applied for Computer Engineering because of the innocent love I had for computers-how they work, how they are programmed, I simply wanted to know more about them. I'll admit I didn't know what i was getting into. It was-is definitely not as easy as I thought and at a point, I thought I made a wrong choice(Looking back now, I definitely did not)

Truly, I started feeling lost in my third year of university as it felt like everyone else knew what they were doing while i was still struggling with the basics, I tried taking multiple courses but they all ended up the same-unfinished. I needed to learn with a proper schedule, timeframe and accountability partners.

I found AltSchool in my quest for a program that meets all these requirements. Thankfully at the same time, I also found a philanthropist that was offering full/half scholarships for women interested in the program. This was an opportunity I could not miss.

I am currently juggling university and Altschool, a hard but necessary task. Thanks to my line of study, I've found that absolutely no knowledge is wasted. The skills I have learnt and I am learning at AltSchool makes my school work easier to grasp due to similar topics and concepts and vice versa. I no longer feel completely lost, I feel like I have finally found a path I walk on.

I am hopeful that by the end of my AltSchool journey, I would be a confident front-end engineer and be able to tackle any project(s) assigned to me as well as use my skills to build projects and solve problems. I am excited to see what the future holds for me.
